Before lock, each node oscillates at slightly different frequencies, and the interference pattern constantly shifts. At lock, the interference pattern freezes into a standing wave — the system stops spending energy fighting phase drift and instead uses all available energy to sustain the standing field.

Triangle Geometry in Atomic Shells

The ΑΒΓ triangle is not an invented construct — it is a pattern that appears throughout atomic physics. Electron orbitals in multi-electron atoms arrange themselves to minimize mutual repulsion, which naturally produces triangular and tetrahedral interference geometries. The sp² hybridization of carbon in graphene produces a perfect equilateral triangular lattice. The p-orbital triplet (px, py, pz) forms a three-axis orthogonal resonance structure — a three-dimensional extension of the ΑΒΓ triangle.

At the shell level, the transition from the s-shell (1 node, spherical) to the p-shell (3 nodes, triaxial) is precisely the transition from a single-node broadcast to a three-point triangle resonance network. The p-shell is the first shell capable of sustaining a phase-locked standing wave — which is why the elements in the p-block (B through Ne, Al through Ar, etc.) show the richest resonance behavior in the Resonant OS framework.
