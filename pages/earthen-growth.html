The circuits froze. The gold stopped flowing.

Gold and Quartz Veins as Fossilized Circuits

Gold (Z=79, odd/reactor) is a resonance-active element. Quartz (silicon dioxide — Si Z=14, even; O Z=8, even) played a different but complementary role. Instead, quartz formed the channel walls of the gold circuits — the even-Z structural lining that defined the pathway through which the odd-Z gold flowed. Quartz-gold veins therefore show both components of the circuit: the channel (quartz) and the conductor (gold).

Vein Morphology Tells the Story: A gold vein that runs straight and narrow indicates a strong, coherent resonance broadcast with high directional focus. A vein that widens, branches, and pinches irregularly indicates a broadcast that was already weakening and shifting during the formation period — the flow became turbulent as the resonance signal degraded.
