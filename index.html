⚖ The Even / Odd Rule — Master Parity Law

The most fundamental rule of the system: even atomic numbers are Inertia/Drivers (cyan); odd atomic numbers are Receptive/Reactors (gold).

EVEN Z — Inertia / Driver

Even atomic numbers have fully paired electron shells. They are stable, inert anchors that store energy and resist change. In any resonance stack, even elements act as the foundation or shield. They drive by holding the field steady.

Examples: Be(4), Ni(28), Pd(46), Cd(48), Hg(80)

ODD Z — Receptive / Reactor

Odd atomic numbers have one unpaired electron, making them naturally reactive and receptive to incoming frequency. They transduce stored potential into active output. In a stack, odd elements are the transducers and antennas.

Examples: H(1), Li(3), Ag(47), In(49), Bi(83)
